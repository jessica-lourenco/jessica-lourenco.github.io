IMAGE RECOGNITION AND CLASSIFICATION WITH CURRENCY NOTES
  • I built a convolutional neural network (CNN) using a sequential model in Keras to classify images of US Dollars and images of Indian Rupees.
  • The dataset consisted of labeled data of 48 images of real currency notes taken in various viewpoints and some images rotated in order to better train the model to recognize and classify the currency notes. The images of the US Dollars were taken by me and the images of the Indian Rupees were taken from a Kaggle Dataset - Indian currency notes.
  • To prepare the data, each image was resized to 140 x 140 pixels in order for the images to have the same shape and converted to a gray-scale image format like the one below. The training data was created by iterating through each image of US Dollars and Indian Rupees, adjusting the size of images and adding the classification of 0 for US Dollar and 1 for Indian Rupee.


  • The sequential model included:
    • Two convolution layers with Conv2D parameters including a learning of a total of 256 filters, a window of 3x3, and the input shape. The layers also include ReLU activation and max pooling of size 2 x 2.
    • A flattening layer that transforms a 2D feature map to a 1D vector.
    • Dense convolution layers where the first one consists of 64 nodes and ReLU activation and the second one of 1 node with final activation as sigmoid to be able to predict the probability as an output of 0 and 1.
